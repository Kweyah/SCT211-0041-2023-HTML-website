Plain Rice Recipe

Rice for one person

Ingredients

Item Quantity
Rice 1 cup
Water 2 Cups
Oil 2 tablespoons
Salt 2 tablespoons

Instructions

  • Rinse the Rice:
    • the rice in a fine-mesh strainer or sieve and rinse it under cold running water until the water runs clear. This helps remove excess starch and prevents the rice from becoming too sticky. Measure the Water: For most types of long-grain white rice, the standard water-to-rice ratio is 2:1. So, for 1 cup of rice, you'll use 2 cups of water. If you prefer slightly firmer rice, you can reduce the water to 1.75 cups.
  • Boil the Water:
    • In a medium-sized saucepan with a tight-fitting lid, bring the water to a boil. Add salt and oil if desired.
  • Add the Rice:
    • Once the water is boiling, add the rinsed and drained rice to the pot. Stir briefly to ensure the rice is evenly distributed in the water.
  • Simmer:
    • Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with the lid, and let the rice simmer. Do not lift the lid during cooking.
  • Cook:
    • Allow the rice to simmer for about 7-10 minutes. Cooking time may vary slightly based on your stove and the type of rice you're using, so you can check the package instructions as a general guideline.
  • Check for Doneness:
    • After the cooking time, remove the pot from the heat, and let it sit, covered, for an additional 3-4 minutes. This allows the rice to steam and finish cooking.
  • Fluff and Serve:
    • Using a fork, gently fluff the rice to separate the grains. Serve it as a side dish or as a base for other dishes.

Serving Suggestion: Serve the plain white rice as a base dish for side dishes such as meat or vegetables
